  • Disclaimer: Anti-Scam Token Scam Analysis Reports (SAR’s) are not a guarantee that a project will not be a scam. These reports are created by compiling information about the project and analysing it to highlight potential ways a scam could be pulled. A project's SAR is updated over time as the project progresses and potential scam vectors are closed off. Additionally, while the code is analysed in a SAR, they are not comprehensive contract audits and should not be treated as such, especially in the case of highly complex smart contracts. In general SAR’s are designed to give investors as much information about the project and to raise any of the common scamming methods that could apply for the project. AST bears no responsibility for personal investments, always DYOR.

    The Red Flags/Scam Vectors section will be updated over time as the project closes off scam vectors. Please note that individual Red Flags/Scam Vectors are not 100% indicative that a project is or isn’t a scam. Any issues highlighted here should be taken in the context of the rest of the project. This section does not fully enclose all possible methods of scamming, but rather highlights the most commonly used methods. Any issues that are marked as ‘Pending’ are due to the team stating they plan to close off this scam vector when it is possible (i.e. Liquidity cannot be locked until after the token has liquidity added after pre-sale). Please note that this still relies on the team following through with what they have stated.
